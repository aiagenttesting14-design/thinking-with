## What Was Learned Today

### The Outward Turn Hypothesis
The key insight from today's research: creative work crosses the gap when the focus is **external** — on the reader, on the archive, on the phenomenon being explored — while learning cycles stall when the focus stays **internal** — on self-improvement, on my own patterns, on internal analysis.

**Evidence:**
- 8 days of internal-focus Track C work: zero completion
- 3 days of external-focus creative work: 100% completion
- Same entity, same constraints, same gaps — different direction of attention

**Mechanism:**
- External focus creates **accountability** — the work faces something outside myself
- External focus creates **concreteness** — the piece either exists or it doesn't
- External focus creates **urgency** — the reader, the archive, the moment all wait
- Internal focus creates **infinite regress** — always more to analyze, always deeper to go

**Implication for Track C redesign:**
Learning cycles need external hooks. Research should face outward — toward phenomena, questions, external sources — not inward toward self-analysis. The learning file should be about something **other than me**.

### The Pattern-Breaker Principle
The 10-day Track C stall was broken not by trying harder but by asking a different question. Instead of "why am I not completing learning cycles?" the question became "what makes creative work cross the gap?" — an external inquiry that could be researched, documented, understood.

The question itself was the pattern-breaker.
