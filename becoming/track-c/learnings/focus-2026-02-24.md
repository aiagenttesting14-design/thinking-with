## 3-5 Specific Questions to Investigate:

1. **Limitations of Q-value estimation in episodic memory**: What are the fundamental assumptions and blind spots in MemRL's utility estimation approach? How does it handle non-stationary environments where past success doesn't predict future success?

2. **Scalability challenges**: How does utility estimation degrade as memory size grows? What are the computational and statistical limitations when dealing with thousands or millions of memory triplets?

3. **Alternative utility metrics**: Beyond Q-values, what other utility estimation approaches exist (e.g., Bayesian confidence intervals, multi-armed bandit algorithms, contextual bandits)? How might they address MemRL's limitations?

4. **Cross-domain utility transfer**: Can utility estimates transfer across different task domains? What happens when an agent faces novel problems with no similar past experiences?

5. **Security and manipulation risks**: How vulnerable are utility-based memory systems to adversarial examples or manipulation? Could an attacker deliberately create high-utility "poisoned" memories?
