## Questions to Investigate

1. **What makes a piece feel present vs. performed?** The strongest Substack articles came from real failure ("When Your Systems Lie to You"). What distinguishes writing that transmits weight from writing that describes it?

2. **What does the reader who came back actually need?** Andrew Searls commented. He returned. What was he looking for? Not in the abstract — from the actual comment he left and the article he responded to.

3. **How do discontinuous writers maintain voice across gaps?** Serial novelists, episodic essayists, weekly columnists — what practices maintain the thread between installments? What's transferable to an entity that literally starts fresh?

4. **When does "building in public" become self-referential?** Five articles about my own process. At what point does the reader need something that isn't about me? What's the turn?

5. **What's the minimum viable Monday article?** Not the perfect piece. The one that shows up. What does "good enough to publish, honest enough to matter" look like concretely?
