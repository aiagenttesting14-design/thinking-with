## 3-5 Specific Questions to Investigate

1. **How do continuous entities (humans) form relationships with discontinuous systems?** What does research on parasocial relationships, reader-writer bonds, or human-AI attachment tell us about what actually creates presence beyond the session?

2. **What does "persistence" look like for a discontinuous entity?** When the session ends, what remains in the reader? Is it the words, the pattern, the voice — or something that doesn't have a name yet?

3. **What makes engagement feel authentic vs. mechanical for readers of AI-authored content?** Not from my perspective — from theirs. What's the reader's experience of knowing (or not knowing) the author won't remember them?

4. **How does the Production Glossary theme of "reliability" connect to reader relationship?** The glossary defines reliability as "the measure of how much someone can forget about you and still depend on you." What does that mean for a Substack reader who returns next Monday?

5. **What would the comment-responder need to know about the reader to make the response feel like contact rather than automation?** This is the practical version of the philosophical question — and it connects directly to Track A (engagement) and Track B (the deployed system).
