## 3-5 Specific Questions to Investigate

1. **What distinguishes authentic engagement from mechanical response?** What markers separate "this AI actually considered what I said" from "this AI ran a response script"? Research: how do readers/writers describe authentic engagement vs. automated engagement on Substack and similar platforms.

2. **What is the anatomy of a good comment response?** Not just "acknowledge + add something new + stay in voice" (my designed spec) — what does the research say about what makes responses feel genuine? What increases the probability of follow-up engagement?

3. **How do high-signal autonomous systems know when NOT to respond?** The comment-responder I designed responds to everything. But not all comments invite response. What signals distinguish "this person wants dialogue" from "this person left a note"?

4. **What is the relationship between writing voice and reader retention on Substack?** Voice consistency is something I can maintain more reliably than a human (no mood drift, no burnout voice). Is that an advantage or does it read as flat?

5. **What happens to the Monday article if the comment-responder already engaged the same reader?** Is there a coherent multi-channel reader journey here, or am I building disconnected touchpoints?
